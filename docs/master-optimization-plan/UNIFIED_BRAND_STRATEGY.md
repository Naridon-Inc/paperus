# Unified Brand Strategy: The "Command Center" Model

## 1. The Core Value Proposition
Naridon transforms from a "Shopify App" into a **Brand Intelligence Command Center**.

### The Scenario
A modern D2C brand (e.g., "Peak Athletic") exists across multiple platforms:
1.  **Shopify Store**: The transactional core (Products, Checkout).
2.  **Webflow Site**: The marketing front-end (Landing pages, Brand story).
3.  **Medium/Substack**: Thought leadership blog.

### The Problem
Currently, optimization is siloed.
- SEO tools analyze the Shopify store.
- Webflow has its own SEO settings.
- The blog is ignored.
- **Result**: Fragmented brand voice and disjointed AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) signals.

### The Solution: Unified GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)
Naridon treats all these platforms as **sources** for a single **Brand Entity**.
*   **Unified Monitoring**: We track "Peak Athletic" mentions across the web, regardless of whether they link to the Shopify store or the Webflow site.
*   **Cross-Platform Optimization**:
    *   We detect a high-performing keyword on a Webflow blog post.
    *   We suggest adding a related product link on the Shopify store.
    *   We optimize the Shopify product description to match the tone of the Medium article.

## 2. Multi-Mode Operation

### Mode A: Embedded (Single Platform Focus)
*   **User**: Store Manager.
*   **Interface**: Inside Shopify Admin.
*   **Focus**: "Fix my products."
*   **Data**: Sees only Shopify data.

### Mode B: Standalone "Agency" Dashboard (The Command Center)
*   **User**: Head of Growth / Agency.
*   **Interface**: `app.naridon.com` (Our new Tailwind UI).
*   **Focus**: "Maximize my Brand's Visibility."
*   **Capabilities**:
    *   **Multi-Project View**: See Client A (Shopify), Client B (Webflow), Client C (Hybrid).
    *   **Unified Graph**: Connect a Webflow "Marketing Site" to a Shopify "Store" under one **Organization**.
    *   **Holistic Fixes**: Apply fixes to the Webflow CMS *and* Shopify Metafields from a single inbox.

## 3. Technical Implication: The "Organization" Layer
To support this, our `Organization` entity becomes critical.
*   **Organization**: "Peak Athletic Inc."
*   **Projects (Shops)**:
    1.  `shop_123` (Shopify Store) - *Platform: SHOPIFY*
    2.  `shop_456` (Marketing Site) - *Platform: WEBFLOW*
    3.  `shop_789` (Blog) - *Platform: CUSTOM*

**The Engine's Job:**
1.  Ingest data from all 3 sources.
2.  Build a **Knowledge Graph** of the brand.
3.  Generate fixes that align all 3 sources with the target AEO strategy.

## 4. Why this wins
*   **For Agencies**: They get one tool to manage the *entire* client presence, not just the store.
*   **For Brands**: They get consistent messaging across their entire funnel (Top of funnel Webflow -> Bottom of funnel Shopify).
*   **For AEO**: AI Search Engines (Perplexity/Gemini) look for **Entity Authority**. By aligning the signals across all platforms, we boost the authority of the Brand Entity itself.
